Commercial Conveyancing

The lending environment has changed significantly over the past twenty four months and caution prevails in most lenders’ attitudes towards risk.  Whilst the competitive pressure to lend the same volumes in the current market place may have diminished as a result of the credit crunch we understand the need for lenders to be ‘open for business’; to continue to lend in a controlled and risk based manner whilst still exceeding customers’ expectations in providing a smooth and efficient service.

Our Commercial Conveyancing team is made up of experienced solicitors who have the in depth knowledge of the legal issues which arise in relation to commercial lending.  Inter-action between our lawyers and the lenders relationship managers is key in moving forward each and every deal and clients can expect to receive sound and practical commercial advice to assist with every point in the decision making process. Our service includes:

  • acting for the Bank only in relation to the acquisition or financing of properties or land to be secured on commercial terms;
  • carrying out full due diligence to provide lenders with a clear Certificate of Title in respect of subject properties by obtaining a full suite of title documentation and replies to enquiries from borrowers’ solicitors.  Includes reporting fully to the lender on rights, encumbrances, leases/tenancies, planning, environmental and any other matters relating to the Property which could adversely impact upon the lender’s security;
  • providing legal opinion on borrower vehicle including its power to borrow and ensuring that the proposed security does not breach existing obligations of the borrower ie negative pledges;
  • assisting with negotiation of facility letters and recommendations as to security to be taken (legal charges, debentures, charges on cash, assignments of rental income, cross company guarantees, director’s guarantees) including a consideration as to whether the security is lawful eg under the Companies Act 2006 and any potential risk to the validity of the security eg Insolvency Act 1986;
  • ensuring the valid execution of the security documentation making certain that appropriate meetings of directors/shareholders have been held and resolutions passed.

For further information, please contact Partner, Alexandra Westwood.
