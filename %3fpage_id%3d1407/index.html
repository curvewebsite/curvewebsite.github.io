Title Rectification

Unregistered mortgages have been a significant problem across the industry for many years. Put simply, an unregistered title means:

  • no security;
  • the Lender is unable to repossess;
  • priority can be lost to a subsequent third party charge;
  • possible fraud or negligence on the part of the conveyancer may go undiscovered.

Where a lender has not had in place a rigorous post-completion checking process, it also means the lender cannot be sure of the value of a particular mortgage portfolio, as a proportion of the mortgages are likely to be unsecured. 

We have assisted many lenders with large scale portfolio audits, establishing which accounts remain unregistered and taking the necessary steps to rectify the position and secure the mortgage. 

We also work alongside many lender post-completion teams on ‘business as usual’ cases, acting as an escalation point for individual cases where the original conveyancer fails to register the mortgage. Where the failure to register arises as a result of the conveyancer’s negligence, we pursue this claim through our professional negligence team. Where a third party’s actions may be preventing registration, the matter can be transferred to the property litigation team.

Discharge in Error

There may be occasions where a charge which has not been redeemed has accidentally been removed from the title register by the chargeholder.  Such occurrences may happen, for example, when a chargeholder has more than one charge but only one has been redeemed or there are two charges with the same date, only one of which has been redeemed but they have both been removed.

We provide a prompt reinstatement service to ensure that the charge is re-registered at the Land Registry as quickly as possible.

Lost Unregistered Title Deeds

Where the lender has mislaid or lost a packet of unregistered title deeds in its custody, our team will reconstitute the title by drafting the necessary documentation and carrying out sufficient title investigation to register the title for the first time at the Land Registry.
