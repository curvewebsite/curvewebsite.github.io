Tweet to eat cake (and we'll donate money to charity)

Wednesday, 19th December, 2012
Pete outlines the latest innovative creation we have integrated into our site, which also helps us to support the homelessness charity Crisis UK.

At Curve, we try and find time to put together new products outside of client work, to the extent that we’re now working towards barricading off one day a week for the entire team to explore and progress some of our internal projects a little more.

We focus on ideas that are simple, relatively quick to turn into an initial prototype, and importantly that have some kind of philanthropic angle. We specifically like to work in an agile way on these projects, as they are perfectly suited to an agile workflow. We’re also looking to arrange some weekend sprints in 2013 - so if this sounds like your cup of tea, let us know.

The CurveCake Project

A recent project which we are iterating through our agile workflow is the CurveCake. The current iteration is in the sidebar (on desktop), in the footer (on mobile), and also has a dedicated page on our website. It is a kind of social and somewhat charitable experiment. The concept is that anyone who tweets publicly, and includes the hashtag #CurveCake within their tweet will eat a slice of the displayed cake, and each slice eaten, Curve donates £1 to the UK homelessness charity Crisis. We’ve set a goal of £100 on our Just Giving page for the CurveCake, and manually update the donations on Just Giving every 10 slices “tw-eaten”, if you will.

So that’s about all there is to it. You’re free to also go over and choose to donate your own hard-earned cash to Crisis if you’re feeling full of goodwill etc, but I think it makes more sense from your point of view to get us to do the donating, and all you have to do is tweet to eat our CurveCake by including the hashtag #curvecake in any public tweet.

Of course, we’ve got some great plans to extend this project much further. Bottom line is we’re really trying out interesting, innovative ways to donate money to charity whilst giving ourselves creative challenges. If you’re one of those kind of people, get in touch, comment, tweet us and we’ll find a way for you to get involved.

Oh, and have a Merry Christmas!
