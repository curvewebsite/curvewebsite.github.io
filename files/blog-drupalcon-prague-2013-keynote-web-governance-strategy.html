DrupalCon Prague 2013 Keynote - Web Governance & Strategy

Wednesday, 25th September, 2013

The following is a live-stream (in reverse chronological order) hosted by Craig and Pete that took place during the ‘Keynote: Web Governance & Strategy’ session at DrupalCon Prague on Wednesday 25th September 2013

Lisa wraps up questions and answers with a note about working in multilingual projects, and how this can help smaller organisations mature and their processes through the default tighter organisation requirements of these kind of projects.
This is followed by a big thank you from Lisa and an applause from the attendees.
-- 10:10

Back to decision making. Often there are many people round the table, where decisions need to be made. If some of those people do not understand the problem, you will often end up with a stalemate position, ending up with the wrong decisions being made.
-- 10:08

Questions and answers with Lisa.
-- 10:02

Lisa with an analogy for an answer: What's better? Water or Air?
This relates to the importance of many aspeects of web technologies and systems such as metadata, taxonomies, through to user interfaces and databases.
-- 10:05

Lisa sums up with some words of wisdom:
"When decisions aren't made, they are being made.
There's no such thing as doing nothing."
-- 09:59

Great talk, very inspiring. Thank you!
(Big round of applause).
-- 10:00

The work we are doing will not make any difference if the web is not open. Our values as a community will support this.
-- 09:57

Enable collaboration through facilitating people that may not understand the languages being used by formulating standards.
Reference to music/singing groups that offer different ways of articulating music, in order to enable collaboration with people that do not understand or read traditional musical annotations.
-- 09:55

Strategy and policy - fundamentally, in organisations and communities (specifically the Drupal community), we need to be more targetted about who is making decisions and how the decisions are being made.
-- 09:51

How do we govern now?
Keep your core. A re-iteration of what she mentioned earlier: Nobody owns anything and everybody owns everything.
Around governance: these are the key terms lisa is discussing: Team, Strategy, Policy, Standards.
-- 09:48

What is happening now - how have we got here? In terms of the web and various trends. Lisa is referencing how we've changed our attitudes and become more serious about web projects.
It's good to stay organic, but it doesn't come from just 'wanting' to do it. We need to "Be brave" and stand up for what we're doing, by being more pro-active about governance.
-- 09:45

Sometimes deviations from the norm are beautiful. References to art, food (a non-perfect looking strawberry).
It's all about perspective. A cancer cell can 'look' beautiful.
-- 09:40

Not only Drupal has frameworks and rules. Lisa proves that she is cool, and shows some less IT-focussed examples: Haiku, Music (and some focus on Jazz, and even in chaotic improvisations there is structure on top of some form of framework.
Also nature - the organic rules that continue to evolved and provide a balance in ecosystems, and life.
-- 09:37

Lisa is touching on the topic of internet access, and talk of it arguably being a human right.
-- 09:31

Lisa: Governance is a framework for establishing accountability.
Because more people are involved in an open community, this means that eveything needs a higher level of clarity to assist in the decision-making process.
On a side-note, decision making seems to be a hot topic at this DrupalCon, it's surfaced time and time again in many conversations I have had so far.
-- 09:29

The topic of her talk is the focus: The paradox of open growth. Open is everyone and everything.
The community is like a ginormous fungus :)
(Cue a few giggles from the crowd)
One of the analogies are: A bunch of good genes in a stable environment - refering to a 4-mile wide fungus that was discovered in Oregon around 2007. This is when Lisa had her 'apple on the head' moment.
-- 09:26 

Lisa is talking about the issues that are often encountered in organisations regarding ownership of websites. We've come across that a few times!
-- 09:21

Lisa Welchmann now hits the stage. She's excited to be here!
Currently she's covering her history in web design. She started in 1996 at Cisco Systems.
-- 09:17

More thanks to sponsors, not just financial, but also time and effort put into arranging various events and other activities, of particular note the education slant that Drupal is leaning more towards.
Brian House (Acquia) now on stage, talking through some new products including Acquia Cloud Free - Sandbox developer environment.
-- 09:13

Some additional information about upcoming events and socials throughout the rest of DrupalCon. Geocaching in Prague tonight and Drupal Trivia night (tomorrow - Thursday 25th) are highlights picked out.
-- 09:10

Some talk of the Drupal supporting partners plan and presenting the logos of many of the organisations that have put real money into the Drupal Association, and how this is being input into the community.

Introductions and a little bit of crowd prepping from some Drupal Association ladies. Woo! It's Wednesday. Thanking the community for all the time and energy that we all put in to Drupal as a whole.
-- 09:02

We have a live blog stream starting right now with the second keynote at DrupalCon Prague this week.
-- 09:00

Keynote: Web Governance & Strategy
